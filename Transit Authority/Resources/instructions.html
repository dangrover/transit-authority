As promised, here's the first prototype release of Transit Authority.

For those unacquainted, Transit Authority is a casual simulation game where the object is to build and run metro systems in cities around the world.

With your feedback, once the mechanics and basic workings are solidified, I'll be finding a designer/art director to get the level of visual polish to what it needs to be.

I'd love to hear your thoughts! Did you understand the game? What confused you? Is it a weird concept for a game? What would make it more fun? Should it be more realistic? Less? What cities would you like to see featured in it? What direction would you take it in?

Thanks!

Dan

MAJOR KNOWN ISSUES

  • Handicap ramp upgrade does nothing.
  • There is no limit to the amount of debt your agency can take on.
  • Can scroll outside the map. Crash when placing things outside the map.
  • Tracks are straight lines. In the real game, they'll be curvy!
  • No saving or restoring games for now.
  • No way to change the number of trains running at night
  • Weird pixel cracks when you zoom and scroll

MAJOR OPEN QUESTIONS

  • What is the sense of progression? How does the player grow between cities? What do we unlock/discover?
  • Should there be disasters/strikes/delays? What does the player do to prevent them? How do they respond to them?
  • Should finance be a lesser aspect of the game? Are subsidies superflous? Should operational costs be surfaced and explored better?
  • What sort of stats and analytics should be available, and how should they be presented?
